# Research Brief: China Drone + AI Crop Inspection

> **Why:** You believe China already has SkyInspect-like tech. Good. Let's map exactly who, what, and where the gaps are. If nobody has the full stack, we have the moat. If somebody does, we know what to copy and what to leapfrog.

## 1. DRONE MANUFACTURERS (The Hardware Layer)

### DJI (Shenzhen) — The obvious starting point
**Research targets:**
- DJI Agras T50 / T25 — crop spraying drones. Do they have inspection modules?
- DJI Mavic 3 Multispectral — captures NDVI/NDRE crop health maps. Does it do **physical sampling** or only imaging?
- DJI P4 Multispectral — older model, widely used in China. Same question.

**Key question:** Does DJI have a **robotic arm / physical sampler** attachment? Or are they purely optical?

### XAG (Guangzhou) — DJI's biggest competitor
**Research targets:**
- XAG P100 Pro / V50 Pro — agricultural drones
- XAG "XAG Crop Vision" or similar AI analytics platform
- Do they offer automated soil/plant tissue sampling?
- Their "XAG Cloud" — what's the data pipeline?

### EAVision (Nanjing) — AI + drone crop scouting
**Research targets:**
- Founded by Stanford AI lab alums
- Claims "AI-powered crop diagnosis" via drone imagery
- Do they go beyond NDVI? Any physical sampling?

### TerraDrone / TerraLoupe — Japanese but big in China
- Skip unless they have unique tech.

**Research exactly:**

**Research targets:**

## 6. REGULATORY & SCALE ENVIRONMENT

**Why this matters:** China's drone regulations are looser than EU/US. If they're not doing it there, the tech gap is real.

**Research targets:**
- China Civil Aviation Administration (CAAC) drone rules for agriculture — how easy is BVLOS (beyond visual line of sight)?
- How many agricultural drones are registered in China? (Estimates: 200,000+ by 2025)
- Average farm size in China vs. Bulgaria/EU — does drone sampling even make sense for 0.5-hectare plots?

**Key insight to verify:**
> China's farms are tiny (avg 0.6 hectares). Drone-based physical sampling might not be economical at that scale. EU farms (avg 16 hectares) are the perfect size. **This is our geographic moat.**
